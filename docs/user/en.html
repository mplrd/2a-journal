1. Welcome to the Trading Journal

Trading Journal is a web application designed for traders who want to centralize, analyze and improve their practice. The tool brings together in one place all your operations: pending orders, ongoing trades, closed positions, and the history imported from your brokers.

Who is this manual for?

  • To the beginner trader who is starting their journal and wants to understand the application's logic.
  • To the experienced trader who wishes to migrate their history from MT4, MT5, cTrader, FTMO or FXCM.
  • To the evaluated trader (prop firm) who wants to manage their challenge with drawdown, profit target and phases.

What you will be able to do after reading:

  • Create multiple accounts (demo, personal, prop firm) with their risk parameters.
  • Enter your orders and trades manually, or import hundreds of operations in a few clicks.
  • Build your own dictionary of setups and custom fields (mood, timeframe, conviction…).
  • Track your P&L, win rate, R/R and equity curve on detailed charts.
  • Share your ideas in one click on WhatsApp, Telegram, X or Discord.

Requirements & access

  • Browser: Chrome, Edge, Firefox or Safari up to date.
  • Application address: the one communicated to you (by your company, your administrator, or the one you use locally).
  • Account: a valid email address to sign up.
  • Data to import (optional): an export of your broker history in Excel, CSV or XML format.

First Steps in 5 Minutes

The minimum path to start recording your trades:

  • Click Sign Up and create your account with an email and password (at least 8 characters including an uppercase letter, lowercase letter and digit).
  • Create your first trading account (name, type, currency, initial capital).
  • Take a look at your list of assets: NASDAQ, DAX, EUR/USD, Bitcoin… are already created for you.
  • Enter your first trade (account, symbol, direction, price, size, stop loss and targets).
  • Open the Dashboard to see your first statistic appear.
Tip Getting started is guided by an onboarding that restricts navigation to useful steps until your account is operational. As long as no trading account exists, only the "Accounts" and "My Account" pages are accessible.
Demo Account To explore the application without creating an account, a demo dataset is included with the project. Sign in with the email demo@2a.journal and password Demo*123: you'll find 3 pre-created accounts (FTMO Challenge, Personal Account, MFF Evaluation), assets, setups, a custom field Trend, and dozens of trades.

2. Creating Your User Account

Sign up is free and instant. You can use the application immediately after creation, even if email verification is still pending.

  • On the home page, click Sign Up.
  • Enter your first name, last name, email and password.
  • Submit: a confirmation email will be sent to you.
  • Click the link in the email to verify your address (you can also do this later, a banner will remind you).
Sign up page — creating a new user account
Secure Password The password must contain at least 8 characters, including one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, and one digit. It is stored irreversibly and can never be read, even by application administrators.

Sign In & Security

To sign in, enter your email and password on the login page. Your session stays active automatically while you use the application — no need to keep signing back in. When you click on Sign Out, all your sessions open on other devices are closed at the same time.

Lockout After Multiple Failed Attempts After 5 failed login attempts on the same account, it is locked for 15 minutes and an alert email is sent to you. The lockout lifts automatically, or immediately after a password reset.

Forgot Password

  • On the login page, click Forgot Password?
  • Enter your email and submit.
  • You receive a link valid for 1 hour in your mailbox.
  • Click the link and choose a new password.

A password reset automatically invalidates all your active sessions: you will need to sign in again on all your devices.

My Profile & My Preferences

Click your avatar in the top right, then click My Account. The page offers four tabs:

  • Profile — your personal information, timezone, default currency, theme.
  • My Assets — the list of your traded symbols (see section 5).
  • My Setups — your dictionary of recurring strategies (see section 6).
  • Custom Fields — your custom fields (see section 7).

4. Managing Your Trading Accounts

A trading account represents an account at a broker, a prop firm, or a demo account. You can create as many as you want: each trade is attached to a specific account, which allows analyzing your performance by account.

Create a New Account

  • Go to Accounts in the sidebar.
  • Click + New Account.
  • Enter the main information: name, type, currency, initial capital.
  • (Optional) Specify the broker, maximum drawdown and profit target.
  • Submit by clicking Save.

Prop Firm Account Specifics

If you choose the Prop Firm type, an additional Phase field appears:

  • Challenge Evaluation phase: you must reach the target without exceeding drawdowns.
  • Verification Second phase, more moderate, which validates your consistency.
  • Funded Funded account: your gains are real and shared according to the defined percentage.

Edit or Archive an Account

  • Click on the account line you wish to modify.
  • The form opens pre-filled. Modify the desired values.
  • Confirm with Save.

To archive an account (for example at the end of a failed challenge), click the trash icon and confirm. The account won't appear longer in the lists but its data remains viewable in history.

  • Click + New Asset.

  • Confirm.

What Is Point Value?

The point value allows the application to convert your points gained or lost into a monetary amount. A trade at +110 points on NASDAQ (point value = 20) with 1 lot is worth 110 × 20 × 1 = 2,200 USD. Without a correct value, your P&L will be calculated in points only, not in money.

Quick Creation When you enter a trade or order and the symbol doesn't exist yet in your list, you can create it directly from the form: click the + to the right of the Symbol selector, the new symbol will be automatically selected after creation.

8. Recording My Operations

The application distinguishes two types of operations:

  • An order: an intention to take a position at a given price. It is pending until it is executed.
  • A trade: a position actually open on the market.

An order, once executed, automatically becomes a trade. You can also enter a trade directly without going through an order (for example to log a trade taken in reaction to an event).

Create an Order

  • Go to Orders in the sidebar.
  • Click + New Order.
  • Enter: account, direction (Buy/Sell), symbol, entry price, size.
  • Enter your stop loss (SL) and break-even (BE) levels.
  • Ajoutez un ou plusieurs objectifs (TP) en cliquant sur + Ajouter un objectif.
  • Enter setups, any notes, and an expiration if needed.
  • Confirm with Create.
Trades page - history of open and closed trades
Live Preview While you fill in the form, a share preview appears at the bottom of the form. It displays in real time the text that will be used to share your order.

Calculs automatiques

The application calculates for you:

  • The SL price based on stop points and direction.
  • The BE price based on break-even points.
  • The price of each TP based on target points.
  • The projected R/R (ratio of first target / stop).

Good to Know The columns displayed on the Trades page are: Account, Symbol, Direction, Entry Price, Size, Setup, Remaining Size, Opened, Status, P&L, plus one column for each custom field you've defined (e.g. Trend).

Create a Trade Directly

  • Go to Trades in the sidebar.
  • Click + New Trade.
  • Enter: account, direction, symbol, entry price, size, opening date.
  • Indiquez SL, BE, et un ou plusieurs objectifs (TP).
  • Enter your setups, notes, and custom fields.
  • Confirm with Create.

Trade Lifecycle

A trade goes through several statuses (labels as displayed in the Status column):

StatutSignification
OpenOpen position, no partial exit taken.
SecuredAt least one partial exit taken (e.g. ½ position taken at TP1, rest protected at BE).
ClosedAll exits taken, position is closed.

Sorties partielles & gestion du break-even

The application handles partial exits finely:

  • On a trade row, click Next Target.
  • The application automatically detects the next expected exit: BE not yet taken, otherwise first untouched TP, etc.
  • The close form opens, pre-filled with the exit price and size to close.
  • Confirm: the exit is recorded, remaining size is updated, and the trade becomes Secured or Closed.

If you've defined a BE without size to protect (e.g. "move SL to BE without reducing position"), a special "Mark BE reached" button allows you to note that BE was taken without modifying the size.

Edit or Transfer a Position

The Positions page groups all your operations (pending orders + trades). Vous pouvez :

  • Edit parameters (price, size, SL, TP, setups, notes).
  • Transfer a position to another account (useful for reorganizing after the fact).
  • View the history of status changes (with date, author, and trigger).

9. Import History broker

Instead of manually entering each trade, you can import an export from your broker. The application recognizes several brokers populaires (cTrader, FTMO, FXCM), accepte un format CSV standard downloadable, and even offers a "custom" import where you tell the application what each column corresponds to your file.

Import is duplicate-free: if you import the same file twice, trades already present are automatically detected and ignored.

  • Go to Accounts.
  • On the relevant account row, click the Import icon.
  • Choisissez le format dans la liste : cTrader, FTMO, FXCM, Standard (CSV) ou Other (Manual Mapping).
  • Drag and drop your file or use Browse.
  • Check the preview and correct if needed the symbol mapping.
  • Confirmez l'import.
Import dialog - step 1: choose broker and file

From cTrader

  • Accepted formats: XLSX, CSV.
  • Automatic detection of columns in French or English.
  • Handles partial exits and groups multiple executions on the same position.

From FTMO

  • Accepted formats: CSV, XLSX (FTMO export).
  • Recognizes explicit opening date and duplicate columns in FTMO format.
  • Ideal for Challenge / Funded accounts: use this template to track your challenge alongside the FTMO application.

From FXCM

  • Accepted format: XML SpreadsheetML (Trading Station export).
  • Fusionne automatiquement les lignes par paire (2 lignes = 1 trade) propres au format FXCM.

Standard Format (CSV)

It's a custom CSV template file that you can download directly from the application: it already contains the right columns in the right order. Fill it with your trades and re-import it for a very fast import with no configuration.

Colonnes attendues, dans cet ordre :

  • Symbol — asset code (NASDAQ, EURUSD, BTCUSD…).
  • Direction — BUY ou SELL.
  • Entry Price — entry price.
  • Size — taille de la position.
  • Open Date — date et heure d'ouverture (format AAAA-MM-JJ HH:MM:SS).
  • Close Date — closing date and time (same format).
  • Exit Price — prix de sortie.

All columns must be present. This is the simplest option if you keep your history in a spreadsheet or if your broker doesn't have a dedicated template.

Format « Other (Manual Mapping) »

If your broker is not in the list and your file doesn't colle pas au format Standard, choisissez Other (Manual Mapping). The application reads the headers of your file and suggests, for each field it expects (Symbol, Direction, Entry Price…), to choose manuellement la colonne qui lui correspond.

Vous pouvez aussi configurer :

  • The date format (for example DD/MM/YYYY for French format).
  • The direction values specific to your file (Buy / Sell, Long / Short, Entry / Exit...).

Champs requis : symbole, direction, prix d'entrée, prix de sortie, size, closing date. Optional fields: opening date, P&L, pips, commentaire.

Map My Custom Fields à l'import

Si vous avez défini des champs personnalisés (cf. section 7), une zone dédiée apparaît à l'étape de import configuration in "Other" mode. For each field personnalisé, vous choisissez la colonne du fichier source qui contient sa value: these values will then be automatically recorded on chaque trade importé.

Mapping des symboles The codes used by your broker may differ from those in your dictionary of assets (for example NAS100 at one, NASDAQ at another). The application manages an alias system that memorizes itself over imports: over time, your broker symbols will be resolved automatically.

Synchroniser un broker en direct (cTrader / MT4 / MT5)

Instead of exporting a file and re-importing it regularly, you can connect your account to its broker via its API. The application récupère alors automatiquement les trades clôturés. Deux connecteurs sont disponibles :

ConnecteurPlateformes couvertesOù récupérer les identifiants
cTrader Tous les comptes cTrader openapi.ctrader.com
MetaApi MetaTrader 4 & MetaTrader 5 metaapi.cloud
Fonctionnalité optionnelle If the Sync button doesn't appear on your accounts, it's because automatic synchronization is disabled on your installation. Contact your administrator to enable it.

Step 1 — Prepare Your API Credentials

Pour cTrader

  • Go to openapi.ctrader.com and sign in with your cTrader account.
  • Create an Application: it gives you a Client ID and Client Secret.
  • Authorize the application on your account to get an Access Token.
  • Note your cTrader Account ID (the account number, e.g. 12345678).

Pour MetaTrader 4 / 5 (via MetaApi)

  • Create an account on metaapi.cloud (third-party service that bridges MT4/MT5 servers).
  • Add your MT4 or MT5 account by entering server, login, and trading password — this information stays with MetaApi.
  • Get your MetaApi API Token from the MetaApi dashboard.
  • Récupérez l'ID du compte MetaApi (identifiant de type abc-def-123, différent du numéro MT4/MT5).

Step 2 — Connect the Account to the Journal

  • Go to Accounts in the sidebar.
  • Sur la ligne du compte concerné, click on l'icône synchronisation (flèches circulaires).
  • Dans la fenêtre Connexion broker, choisissez Connecter cTrader ou Connecter MT4/MT5.
  • Fill in the form:
    • cTrader : Client ID, Client Secret, Access Token, Account ID cTrader.
    • MetaApi : Token API MetaApi, ID du compte MetaApi.
  • Click Connect: the application tests the connection and saves it.
Un seul broker par compte Chaque compte de trading ne peut être lié qu'à une seule connexion broker à la fois. If you try to add a second one, the application responds: « Ce compte est déjà connecté à un broker ». Déconnectez l'ancienne avant d'en configurer une nouvelle.

Étape 3 — Lancer une synchronisation

Une fois le compte connecté, un panneau Connexion broker apparaît sur la ligne du compte avec :

  • Le statut actuel : Active, En attente, Erreur, Révoquée.
  • La date de la dernière sync.
  • Les boutons Synchroniser, Historique et Déconnecter.
  • Click Sync.
  • The application retrieves all closed trades since the last sync (incremental sync — a cursor keeps track).
  • Un message confirme le résultat : « X importée(s), Y doublon(s) ignoré(s) ».

Ce que fait la synchronisation pour vous, automatiquement :

  • No duplicates: trades already in the journal are recognized and ignored. You can re-run the sync as many times as you want safely.
  • Creating unknown assets: a symbol not in your My Assets list is created automatically with a default point value — remember to correct it so your P&L is calculated in money, not just points.
  • Broker code recognition: codes specific to your broker (e.g. NAS100) are matched to your assets (e.g. NASDAQ) using the mappings you created during file imports.
  • Synchronisation rapide : seuls les trades plus récents que le dernier import sont récupérés, les syncs successives prennent quelques secondes.

Étape 4 — Consulter l'historique des synchronisations

Click History from the login panel to open the fenêtre Historique des synchronisations. Vous y voyez une ligne par sync avec :

ColonneDescription
DateHorodatage de la synchronisation.
StatutSuccès ou erreur.
RécupérésNombre de deals renvoyés par le broker.
ImportedTrades actually created in the journal.
IgnorésDoublons détectés (identifiant externe déjà présent).
ErreurMessage en cas d'échec (token expiré, compte déplacé…).

Déconnecter un compte

Click Disconnect in the panel then confirm. The encrypted credentials are deleted on the server; your already imported trades obviously remain in the journal. You can reconnect at any time avec de nouveaux identifiants.

Security of Your Credentials Client Secret, Access Token et Token MetaApi sont chiffrés de manière irreversibly on the server before being stored. They are never réaffichés et ne sont utilisés que pendant une synchronisation. Si vous les perdez, il faut en générer de nouveaux chez le broker.

Messages d'erreur fréquents

Message affichéCause probableQue faire
Veuillez renseigner tous les identifiants A form field is empty. Vérifiez que tous les champs sont remplis (Client ID, Secret, Access Token, Account ID cTrader ; ou Token MetaApi + ID du compte MetaApi).

Import fichier vs synchronisation API The two paths complement each other. You can start by importing your historique complet avec un fichier (cf. sections précédentes), puis activer ensuite la synchronisation API pour que les trades à venir entrent automatiquement : la déduplication garantit qu'aucun doublon ne sera créé.

14. FAQ

Que faire si je perds mon mot de passe ?

Cliquez sur Forgot Password ? sur la page de connexion. Vous recevrez un lien valable 1 heure pour le réinitialiser.

Mon compte est verrouillé, comment le débloquer ?

Le verrouillage automatique dure 15 minutes. Vous pouvez aussi reset your password: this immediately unlocks your account.

Is my data synchronized between my devices?

Yes. All your data (accounts, trades, setups, language preferences…) is stored on the server and accessible from all your devices in real time.

Puis-je supprimer définitivement un trade ?

When you delete a trade, it disappears from the application; it remains kept in the background to allow for backups or audits if nécessaire. Il ne réapparaît dans aucune liste ni statistique.

My broker is not in the import list, what should I do?

Use the Generic format: export your trades in CSV from your platform, and the application will let you map the columns manuellement. Pour les imports récurrents, le mapping est mémorisé.

Pourquoi ne vois-je pas mon P&L en euros ?

Check the point value of your symbols in the My Assets tab. Without a correct value, the application displays the P&L in points uniquement, pas en montant monétaire.

Puis-je avoir plusieurs comptes en parallèle ?

Yes, you can create as many accounts as needed (demo, personal, prop firm). Chaque trade est rattaché à un compte unique, et tous les filtres et statistiques respectent ce rattachement.

Comment ne plus afficher un compte terminé ?

Archive it (trash icon). It will disappear from the lists but its data will be kept for your historical statistics.
